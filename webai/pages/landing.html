How it works

The current phase of this project is to collect data. For that we need to extract interactions users are performing on the web, and you can help by adding the script above on your site. It works this way:

1. Users explore your site normally, each second their mouse movements, clicks and scrolls are sent as packets to my server.

2. When collected, the origin page is crawled to extract key metrics from the interaction with the content: specific sections, shapes, colors, all contextualizing the actions performed.

3. After three pages of your site visited, a pop-up invites your visitor to take part in a survey. This allows to map certain profiles with the extracted metrics, and label part of the data.

This process allows linking usage of web-content to a psychological state experienced by the user. Also, we can study these interactions at content-specific levels which could provide insights into improving KPI performances and better design routines for developers.

Many goals such as personalized and relevant content.

This project has many goals. One of them could be to improve a user's experience by providing more personalized and relevant content. By collecting user interactions and predicting user profiles, the AI could tailor the content and recommendations it presents to individual users. This can lead to a more enjoyable and satisfying experience for users, as they are presented with content that is more relevant to their interests and needs. Additionally, the AI may be able to anticipate user needs and proactively present relevant information, further enhancing the user experience.

Protecting user privacy by eliminating the need for invasive cookie tracking.

Cookies are small pieces of data that are stored on a user's device and used to track their online behavior. While cookies can be useful for certain purposes, such as remembering user preferences or providing targeted advertising, they can also be a privacy concern as they can be used to track a user's activities across different websites and build up a detailed profile of their interests and habits. By collecting user interactions and predicting user profiles through other means, this research project aims to eliminate the need for cookies and protect user privacy.

Advance the field of artificial intelligence by training AI's to navigate the web using only web elements.

Currently, AI's are often trained using large datasets, which can be time-consuming and resource-intensive to create and maintain. By collecting user interactions and predicting user profiles from web elements alone, the AI research project aims to develop a new method of training AI's that is more efficient and scalable by working with client-users. This could open up new possibilities for AI applications and potentially lead to the development of more advanced AI systems in the future.
